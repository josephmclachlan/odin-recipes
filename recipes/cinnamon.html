Cinnamon Syrup

Description

This simple recipe adds a huge amount of flavor to any drink. Great with rum, tequila, and bourbon, this syrup will build body anywhere it is used, and can accentuate any drink needing a simple syrup.

The secret to a great cinnamon syrup is not using plain brown sugar! Go the extra mile and find some demerara sugar, turbinado, or panela if you can find it. These much darker sugars will add complexity and depth to the syrup that will translate into any drink it is used with.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 cups of dark brown sugar*
  • 5 cinnamon sticks

Steps

  1. Place water in pot and bring to boil over high heat
  2. Once boiling add 2 cups of sugar and immediately reduce heat to simmer.
  3. Stir until sugar is fully dissolved
  4. Add cinnamon sticks, cover, and simmer for 6 minutes
  5. Remove from heat and allow to steep covered for 6 hours
  6. Add to squirt bottle or jar with cinnamon sticks, and store in refrigerator.
Notes

*If you cannot find demerara or panela sugar, check the hispanic section of your grocery store. If you still cannot locate these sugars, turbinado is a good substitute.

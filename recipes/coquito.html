Coquito Recipe

Description

Most americans think of just one iconic drink when the holiday season arrives; Eggnog. While a great drink, I find it can very quickly be too much of a good thing, and most parties end with half finished cups lying all over the place.

As a Puerto Rican, I have some magic to share with you; Coquito. Gone are eggs that can spoil, and gallons of milk. Replace it with swet, tropical coconut, smooth rum, and all the best spice the West Indies have to offer.

Ingredients

  • 4 Cans of Coco Lopez (60oz)
  • 5 Cans Carnation milk (60oz)
  • 1 can condensed milk
  • teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Paste from 1 pliable vanilla pod*
  • 2 cups of premium aged rum**
  • teaspoon of fresh ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of allspice

Steps

  1. Grind fresh whole spices
  2. Add all ingrediants to blender*** and blend till fully homogenized
  3. Serve in very small glasses
  4. Add sprinkle of ground cinnamon, and a few fresh gratings of nutmeg
  5. Enjoy!
Notes

*Fresh vanilla works best for a deep and rich flavor. Make sure your vanilla pod is pliable and not dry and hard. If not available sub 1 tsp tsp of vanilla paste or extract.

**Do not use spiced rums or cheap swill. Bacardi 8, Don Q Gran Anejo, or even better Ron de Barralito 3 star (if you can find it) are all good affordable options. This is the single most imporant factor on flavor an quality of coquito.

***Using a blender can cause the drink to become unnecissarily frothy. If you have an immersion blender a preferable method would be adding all ingrediants to a pitcher and blending fully with an immersion blender.
